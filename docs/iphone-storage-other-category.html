iPhone 'Other' Storage: What It Is & How to Clear It

You check your iPhone storage and see "Other" or "System Data" using 10-50GB. What is this mystery category and why is it so huge?

What is "Other" Storage?

The "Other" category (called "System Data" in newer iOS versions) includes:

  • Cached files and data
  • Safari browser cache
  • Mail attachments
  • Siri voices
  • System logs
  • App caches
  • Streaming music/video caches

Why Does It Keep Growing?

iOS caches data aggressively to make apps faster. But these caches rarely clean themselves, so they grow over time.

How to Reduce "Other" Storage

1. Clear Safari Cache

Settings > Safari > Clear History and Website Data

2. Offload Unused Apps

Settings > General > iPhone Storage > Offload Unused Apps

3. Delete and Reinstall Large Apps

Apps like Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok build up huge caches. Deleting and reinstalling clears them.

4. Clear Message Attachments

Settings > General > iPhone Storage > Messages > Review Large Attachments

5. Backup and Restore

The nuclear option - backing up and restoring your iPhone can clear stubborn cached data.
